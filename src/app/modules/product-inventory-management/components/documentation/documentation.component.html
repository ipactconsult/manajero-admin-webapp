Documentation


Introduction Product & Stock module is where you can manage your materials (finished product, raw material or service) and purchase them by using the procurement process in order to stock them in different warehouses and managing the stock.


Categories To view categories, go to Product & Stock -> Categories



You can view categories in other view modes.




You can search a category, export categories to Excel or sort them by name.



You can see category details, edit or archive a category.



To create a new category, click on +. The category creation page will open.




Fill the category information and click on "Save Data".



Note that if the category already exists, you will get an error and the category will not be added.
Materials To view materials, go to Product & Stock -> Materials



You can view materials in other view modes.




You can search a material, export materials to Excel, filter by type and category or sort them by name.



You can see material details, edit or archive a material.



To create a new material, click on +. The material creation page will open.




Fill the material information and click on "Save Data".



Note that if the material already exists, you will get an error and the material will not be added.

Procurement Process To create a purchase, go to Product & Stock -> Purchase -> Create Purchase
Note: You can cancel the process or go back to the previous step.



1) In step 1, identify the enterprise needs by selecting the materials to purchase. then click on "Next".



2) a) In step 2, create the purchase requisition. To do so, update the quantity by clicking on .



2) b) Edit "Material Quantity" and click on "Save Data". Then, click on "Next".



2) c) Purchase requisition is created and reviewed by the Finance Team. Its status is set to Pending.
Note: Purchase requisition needs to be approved by the Finance Team in order to go to step 3.



2) d) This is what you get when your purchase requisition is approved. Then, click on "Request For Quotation".



2) e) This is what you get when your purchase requisition is rejected. Click on "Quit" to terminate the process.



3) In step 3, create request for quotations by selecting at least two suppliers and entering the quotation deadline. Then, click on "Next".
Note: It is recommended to select three suppliers.



4) In step 4, you maintain quotations sent by suppliers.



4) a) To do so, select suppliers one by one and update material price by clicking on under "Maintain Price" in Materials.



4) b) Enter "Material Price" Info and click on "Update". Then, click on "Save Data".
Note: You must do that to all suppliers you selected before you proceed to the next step.




4) c) Once done, click on "Next".



5) In step 5, you decide who to purchase materials from.



5) a) To do so, select suppliers to see the offers they provide and compare them to each other.
When you decide the vendor, select the one you have chosen and click on "Next. and confirm you would like to choose the vendor.




6) In the final step, after choosing the right vendor, create purchase order.



6) a) To do so, enter "Receipt Date" when materials are set to be received, click on "Finish" and confirm the purchase order creation.



6) b) The purchase order is created and sent to the finance team to be reviewed.

In Stock To view materials in Stock, go to Product & Stock -> Stock -> In Stock
You are able to see stocked materials, its stock quantity and you get an alert when the stock is below the minimum capacity.





You can view materials in stock in other view modes.




You can search a material in stock, export materials in stock to Excel, filter by type and category or sort them by name.



You can see stocked material details or transfer it from a warehouse to another one.



If you click on TRANSFER, a new popup window will open where you will choose the warehouse the stocked material will be transfered to.



If the quantity in stock reaches below the minimum capacity, then you will get an alert.
You need to replenish the material to maintain the stock.



Once you click on REPLENISH, you are redirected to a replenishment process page of that material.
1) In Step 1, you submit a purchase request to the finance team and it will be reviewed shortly.
Note: The "Quantity Needed" value is set to the difference between the maximum quantity and quantity stocked.
You can decrement it until the difference between the minimum quantity and the quantity stocked.
You can also increment it until the difference between the maximum quantity and quantity stocked.
Once done, click on "Next" to submit a purchase request to the Finance Team.



2) In Step 2, you see the purchase request information including its Pending status.



You can quit the "Replenishment Process" to do other things and you can check if the purchase request is Approved or Rejected by going to
Product & Stock -> Stock -> Replenishment
Note: You can abandon the process by clicking on .


When the "Purchase Request" is Rejected, you have nothing else to do except clicking on .



When the "Purchase Request" is Approved, you are then able to click on to pursue the process.



Once done, you can then proceed by clicking on "Next".



3) In the last step, you create a Purchase Order that will be Sent to the following Supplier and Reviewed by the Finance Team.



Note: Repeat the Steps in Purchase Orders and Reception to Stock the Quantity Bought.
Once done, go to Product & Stock -> Stock -> In Stock to see the updated Quantity in Stock.

Inventories To view inventories, go to Product & Stock -> Stock -> Inventories
In inventories, you are able to see all inventories.


You can view inventories in other view modes.




You can search an inventory, filter inventories by creation date and inventory date, export them to Excel or sort them by reference.



You can view Inventory Details or archive it.



To create an inventory, click on +. The inventory creation process page will open.



1) In Step 1, choose an inventory date for the inventory.



2) In Step 2, update Reel Stock values of every material by clicking on under Reel Stock.





After updating Reel Stock of shown materials, click on "Next" and you will be asked if you would like to do a second counting.
If you click on Yes, you will go to the third step otherwise, you will go to the last step.



3) In Step 3, in case you confirmed to do a second counting, you will have to do the same thing done in Step 2.
Once done, click on "Next" to go to the last step.



4) In Step 4, you have the following inventory information. Click on "Finish" to confirm the inventory creation or "Back" to get to the previous step.



The inventory is created successfully but it has issues.
You have to click on to fix the difference between quantity in stock and reel quantity and update the quantity in stock to its reel stock value.



You have the following inventory details including inventoried materials.
If there is a difference between quantity in stock and reel stock, you have to click under "Action" on so that quantity in stock and reel stock match.



Now that quantities in stock of materials match with reel stock values, you click on to update the stocked quantity in stock value.



The inventory is fixed and an inventory movement is created. You can learn more about inventory movements in "Inventory Movements" section.
As you can see: It shows All Set.
It means that there is no difference between quantities in stock and reel stock values.



If you would like to create another inventory, you can't because you have already created one this month
And you can create another one the next month.
You can only create one inventory per month.
Inventory Movements To view inventory movements, go to Product & Stock -> Stock -> Inventory Movements
In inventory movements, you are able to see all inventory movements that are created after fixing and updating stock quantities in inventories.
An inventory movement can be an entry ("Quantities entered in stock") or an exit ("Quantities exited the stock").



You can view stock entries in other view modes.




You can search an inventory movement, filter inventory movements by date and material, export them to Excel or sort them by reference.



You can view Inventory Movement Details or archive it.

Let us take the example of "Stock Movements".


You can search an archived stock movement, filter stock entries by date and material, export them to Excel or sort them by reference.
